The Journey To West

The world is older than memory itself. Before names were spoken, before kingdoms learned to rise and fall, there was only the endless stretch of silence… and the path that no one truly understands.

They call it the Westward Expanse. Not because it lies to the west—but because everything that enters it is slowly carried away from what it once was. Time does not behave correctly here. Neither do stories.

And yet… the road continues. You find yourself standing where no clear memory begins. No past that feels complete. Only the faint certainty that you have arrived somewhere important—without knowing why.

The land stretches forward in quiet patience, waiting not for movement, but for choice. Ahead, the path divides. Not into directions—but into possibilities. Each one unspoken.

A thought rises within you, unfamiliar yet certain: You are not defined yet. Not by strength. Not by fate. Not even by name. Only by the choice you are about to make.

The air grows still. Even the wind seems to pause, as if listening. And then—a truth settles into you, quiet but undeniable: Before the journey begins… you must decide who is taking it.
